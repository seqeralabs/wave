---
title: Container mirroring
description: Copy a container image to a registry of your choice while preserving its original name and digest.
---

Storing container images in a registry you control improves security, supports policy compliance, and gives faster access. Wave mirroring automates the copy. Wave pulls the image from its source and pushes it to your registry on demand. The image name and digest stay identical to the source.

Mirroring and [container freeze](./container-freezes.mdx) both produce a permanent image in your registry. They differ in identity:

- **Freeze** returns a new, Wave-built image under a name you choose. The tag is a 16-character build hash.
- **Mirror** copies the image byte-for-byte. The manifest, name, and digest all match the source. Any tool that pins the image by digest continues to work.

Choose mirroring when the original image must keep its identity.

Use cases include:

- **Region-specific deployments**: Place containers in the right geographic region to meet data residency requirements or cut latency.
- **Enhanced security**: Serve images from a registry behind a firewall or inside tight networking controls.
- **Cost and performance**: Pull from a cloud-native registry co-located with compute. Egress costs drop compared with pulls from remote registries.
- **Multi-cloud pipelines**: Deploy workflows across AWS, Google Cloud, and Azure. Each cloud pulls from its local registry.

## How it works

1. When a pipeline runs, Wave identifies the target environment and the associated registry.
2. If the image is missing from the target registry, Wave mirrors it. Wave pulls the image from the source and pushes it to the destination. The manifest does not change.
3. Later requests in the same region or account use the mirrored copy. No repeated transfers.

Mirror status and logs are available via the mirror service endpoints:

- `GET /v1alpha1/mirrors/{mirrorId}` — status and metadata.
- `GET /v1alpha1/mirrors/{mirrorId}/logs` — full mirror log output.
